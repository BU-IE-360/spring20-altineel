Introduction¶

The question I will investigate throughout this homework is How the interest rate and USD/TRY currency changes effect house prices in Istanbul in the last decade? Even though there is a correlation between interest rate and USD/TRY currency and there are other major causes on house prices, for the simplicity of this homework Ive ignored those. If interest rate is low people are more willing to investigate their money on real-estate. Also, when interest rate is low, people are more willing to take housing loan. Therefore, the house prices increase due to high demand. On the other hand, high interest rate is an indicator of high inflation rates. That means everythings price increases due to inflation. However, high interest rate mean high mortgage rates and therefore, more people are willing to sell their houses and this increase in supply will cause house prices to fall. Namely, there is a complicated dynamics in between. Because Turkeys economy is foreign-dependent, prices are dependent on the USD/TRY exchange rates. Thus, in its simplest format, we expect the house prices to fall when USD/TRY is low and vice versa.

Analysis on Visualization¶

As shown in the pairplot, Istanbul House Price is highly correlated with USD/TRY exchange rate and also somehow correlated with the interest rate. As mentioned above this analysis require other knowledge about the economical dynamics of Turkey too. So when we take USD/TRY exchange rate at hand, it is better to divide it into two different periods. Namely, when exchange rate is below 4 (almost), we see a highly linear dependency between exchange rate and house prices as expected. However, when exchange rate is above 4, the linear dependency is broken and this might be due to fast deterioration and unreliability in Turkeys economy. The fact that house prices stay stabile even though the exchange rate increases is because people were less willing to buy houses when Turkeys economy was very unreliable. So when we take interest rate at hand, also it is meaningful to divide it into two parts. Before the interest rate is below 10% (when economy somewhat stabile and reliable), the interest rate is somehow stabile in between 5% and 10% and house prices are not very dependent on interest rate but increase anyway. That is also related the fact that house demand in Istanbul increased so much for the last decade and also as mentioned above the house prices are driven by steady but slow increase in exchange rate. When we look at the second part, namely when interest rate is above 10%, this is also signal of economical deterioration and also signal of increase in mortgages so people are less willing to buy house. In this sense we expect the house prices to fall however, the increase in the exchange rate means increase in house prices. However, high interest rate tries to decrease house prices and increase in exchange rate tries to increase house prices thus, the house prices stay somewhat stabile. When we look at the three figures in timeline, we also see that until 2017 (after the coup attempt), the interest rate is somewhat stabile in between 5-10% (the graphic shows interest rate divided by 5 to put figures in the same range) and the house prices steadily increase with the increase in exchange rate. After 2017, the increase in house prices slows and even we start to see some decreases and more volatility. Also even though the exchange rate increase rapidly the house prices cant follow this increase. Meanwhile the interest rate increases up to 23% which causes a huge, unseen decrease in house prices in the 2019. After 2019 wee see that the exchange rate is somewhat stabilized, interest rate rapidly decrease and the house prices started to increase slowly but steadily. When we look at the heatmap we also see the similar analysis. Until the 100th observation (June 2018), the house price increases steadily, the exchange rate also increases but somehow more volatile and the interest rate is somehow stabile in between 5 and 10%. After June 2018, we see a huge jump in exchange rate and interest rate, whereas the house prices stay stabile and decrease a little bit. The correlation matrix also tells us that house prices and exchange rate is highly correlated whereas house prices and interest rate is less correlated.

Analysis on Linear Regression¶

When we put these data into multiple linear regression model, we get the following equation House Prices = -242.39Interest Rate + 1602USD/TRY. That is quite meaningful because we expected to exchange rate has positive effect whereas interest rate has reverse effect on house prices. When we check the predictions and observations we, again, get similar results. When house prices are below 4000TL, the predictions are almost reasonable however, when the house prices are above 4000TL the model predicts them to be much higher than they really are and that is because, as mentioned above, the exchange rate increases rapidly but house prices cant follow this increment. Finally, when we check the simple linear regression plot of exchange rate against house prices, even though the model undervalue the house prices below 4500TL, it is a somehow good exploratory variable and the confidence interval is smaller. When house prices are above 4500Tl the model overvalue the house prices and the confidence interval is much wider. We also see that interest rate is not a good estimator of house prices as exchange rate.

Conclusion¶

The USD/TRY exchange rate is highly correlated with the house prices in Istanbul and very good estimator of it until 2018. Because of the coup attempt and economical unreliability in Turkeys economy after 2018 (preliminary knowledge), the correlation between exchange rate and house prices decrease and even starts to give highly unreliable predictions. After 2019, when the exchange rate is stabilized, the house price starts to increase steadily but slowly. The interest rate is also effective in house prices as expected but not as much as exchange rate. It is reverse effective on house prices and we can see its affect when wee look at 2013. The interest rate decreases rapidly and there is a rapid increase in house prices. Also, in the beginning of 2014 there is a rapid increase in interest rate and wee see that the increase in house prices slows down and there is the first evidence of small decrease in house prices.
